What To Do When Spring Comes!
Warmer days are the perfect time to get a head start outdoors to make sure your lawn and garden are the envy of your neighborhood. Here are a few suggestions:

Nip that crabgrass in the bud. During the next few weeks, put down a coating of crabgrass preventer, use one which also includes lawn fertilizer. By putting coating on your lawn now, you won't need to depend on a crabgrass killer later on. An 18-pound bag will cover about 5,000 square feet and is inexpensive. The best way to apply the preventer is with a broadcast fertilizer spreader.

This is the ideal time to thatch your yard. Thatching pulls out the dead grass so that when you apply grass seed in early spring, the seeds will have good contact with the soil

After thatching, comes plugging. "Plugging is particularly important for soil that is very compact because it breaks down the soil and aids in drainage," said John St. Lawrence of The Home Depot.

Lastly, aerating helps with drainage. Thatchers, pluggers and aerators can be purchased at any Home Depot location.
